Locating a National Collection - Coins & Monasteries

  1. Maps
  2. Coins & Monasteries

This map presents the locations of early mediaeval monasteries in England and Wales, together with the locations of archaeological finds of Early Western coins. The map opens with a view of the south-west of England, where rivers and some postulated Roman ways are also highlighted.

Suggested Tasks

Here are some ideas for things you can try to do with this map:

  • Use the Dataset filter to see the distribution of early monasteries across Great Britain. Use the visualisation mode button (below the zoom button on the right of the map) to switch between Points, Clusters, and Heatmap.
  • Click on a monastery point to see the date at which it was founded, and click through to see any related records - which might include a link to a Wikipedia article, for example.
  • Do you sense any correlation between the two datasets, either in south-west England or across Britain more generally?

Dataset Preparation

Although primarily concerned with the representation of point data, we need Peripleo also to display other types of digitised geometries such as lines and polygons. This map includes such data as underlays. It also demonstrates some considerations that may arise when presenting different types of data, and features that may have multiple types.
